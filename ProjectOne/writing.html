In our first project this year I attempt to visually summarize the collective feeling of anticipation we are all feeling as Covid-19 restrictions begin to loosen and we start out lives face-to-face again. After doing almost 2 years virtually, we have been forced to reconsider our priorities and what is most important to us. There is a feeling of excitement about the future and the endless possibilities of reality that become available with this mindset change, and also with the immersive digital technologies that are available in 2021. The media themselves asks us to reconsider what is possible and take a good look at what we want our future to look like.

Bus Stop Ads on George Street


  • Design in situ on the bus stops along George Street in New Brunswick. Sidewalks are small so people usually have to walk around the bus stop. The bus stop ads are clearly in view when walking down sidewalk. In this setting there is no noise so the use of text is especially important. The vertical format of the bus stop sides do present some issues as the text will have to be reimagined to fit the screen.












Billboards Over the Entrances to Madison Square Garden


  • Digital advertising boards over the sidewalks around Madison Square Garden in New York. This is a very visible space located only a few blocks from Times Square and being a venue that attracts many people from commuters to sports & music fans. These advertisements will be seen at a very large scale but also from afar and therefore cannot be interacted with. The signs are highly visible to cars as well as pedestrian foot traffic on 7th and 8th Ave.









Pre-Showing Movie Theater Ads

  • This video already has a cinematic feel to it and therefore fits well in this space. The format of the wide cinematic screen works well for the dimentions of the video and the content is creative and suitable for all ages. The video will need to be engaging because people will expect to see well-executed and exciting animations in this space.
